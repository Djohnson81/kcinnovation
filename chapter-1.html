The Founders

Kimberly-Clark’s Place in History

It all started in 1872 when four businesspeople from Neenah, Wisconsin pooled their resources to build the Globe mill in Neenah, the first paper mill in the United States to make print and book papers entirely from rags.

 

Over the next 25 years, the business expanded to include 14 mills and sold paper throughout the mid-western US, helping to fuel consumption of paper products within the US economy.

K-C’s heritage pre-dates some of the most important inventions and advancements of the 19th and 20th centuries.

Scott Paper Co. Limited is founded in Philadelphia as a limited partnership on June 2nd, 1874. The partners include brothers Thomas, Irvin and Clarence Scott and their cousins Thomas Seymour and Zerah Hoyt.

In 1878, Kimberly, Clark and other investors form the Atlas Paper Co. and build a large mill in Appleton, Wisconsin. When the mill goes into operation the next year, it is the first in the state to produce paper largely from ground or mechanical pulp.
